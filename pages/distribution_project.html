Green Vigilance: Drone Innovation for Early Crop Disease Detection

Utilizing UAV and UGV systems for early detection of plant diseases in precision agriculture.

Status: Ongoing...

Project Overview

This project integrates UAVs (aerial vehicles) and UGVs (ground vehicles) for early crop disease detection. The system combines aerial data from UAVs with ground-level inspections by UGVs, employing advanced sensors and algorithms to improve the precision and timeliness of disease identification.

System Architecture

The distributed system includes UAVs for broad aerial surveys and UGVs for targeted ground inspections. The UAVs use hybrid depth sensors to gather spatial data, which guides UGVs for precise interventions. Communication between UAVs and UGVs ensures an efficient flow of information and coordination.

Methodologies

Key methods include:

  • Hybrid Depth Sensing: UAVs and UGVs use sensors for a 3D spatial representation of the field.
  • Weighted Least Squares (WLS): Optimizes UGV positioning for accurate ground navigation.
  • Extended Kalman Filter (EKF): Improves UAV navigation and data reliability by integrating GPS and IMU data.

Experimental Results

Simulations show that integrating UAV and UGV data provides accurate disease detection, even under sensor noise. The heatmaps generated from UAV data guide UGVs to high-risk areas, improving intervention accuracy and resource efficiency.
