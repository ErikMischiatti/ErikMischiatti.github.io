Master Thesis Project - Leveraging Acquired Knowledge and Invariant Representations for Cable Manipulation

Development of a demonstration-based learning system for robotic arms, focusing on invariant representations to improve autonomous manipulation of flexible objects. The system leverages vision and reconstruction techniques for complex tasks, validated in realistic scenarios.

Status: Completed

Bidirectional Invariant Representation (DHB)

The DHB framework ensures robust motion trajectory reconstruction, invariant to rotations, translations, scaling, and temporal changes. It enables robots to adapt learned behaviors to diverse scenarios, enhancing precision in tasks like cable manipulation.

By leveraging DHB, the project demonstrates how motion can be generalized and applied to new scenarios, ensuring reliable performance even under varying environmental conditions.

Experimental Setup

The experimental environment includes:

  • A Franka Emika Panda robotic arm, equipped with an Intel RealSense D435i camera for precise object manipulation.
  • A task board system featuring 3D-printed components and Aruco markers for task definition and localization.

The setup enables the robot to perform complex tasks such as cable insertion, leveraging vision-based detection and invariant representation techniques.
