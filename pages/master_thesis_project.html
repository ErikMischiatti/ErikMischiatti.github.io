Robotic Manipulation System: Modular Assembly and Deformable Object Handling

Advanced system enabling autonomous manipulation of rigid and deformable objects, leveraging vision-based detection and invariant representations. Designed to tackle complex tasks such as cable manipulation in modular environments.

Status: Completed

Bidirectional Invariant Representation (DHB)

The DHB framework enables the transformation of motion trajectories into an invariant space, ensuring adaptability to various spatial configurations. This approach guarantees precision and reliability in manipulation tasks.

By leveraging DHB, the system can generalize trajectories to align with diverse start and goal configurations, enhancing performance even in variable experimental setups.

Experimental Setup

The system integrates cutting-edge hardware and software for robotic manipulation:

  • Franka Emika Panda robotic arm with Intel RealSense D435i camera for precision manipulation and vision-based detection.
  • A modular assembly board equipped with 3D-printed components and Aruco markers for task localization.
  • Advanced software integration utilizing ROS, OpenCV, and DHB-based trajectory planning.

This configuration enables autonomous handling of complex scenarios, such as inserting Ethernet cables and interacting with modular tools.

Test Scenarios and Results

Key scenarios tested during the project, demonstrating the system's capabilities and identifying areas for improvement:

  • Clip Task: Successfully inserted and manipulated Ethernet cables in modular clips with high precision.
  • NIST-Inspired Path: Attempted to follow a complete modular path, highlighting challenges in handling deformable objects and joint limits.
  • Flexibility Tests: Reconfigured the board and tools to validate adaptability and optimize joint reachability.

Key observation: The system excelled in precise manipulation tasks but requires enhancements for handling more complex and dynamic scenarios.

Conclusions and Future Directions

The project demonstrated effective manipulation capabilities in controlled scenarios and laid a solid foundation for further developments:

  • Integration of dual-arm configurations for enhanced cable manipulation.
  • Implementation of advanced perception systems, including real-time 3D reconstruction and tactile sensing.
