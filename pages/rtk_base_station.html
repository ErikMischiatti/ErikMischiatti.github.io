RTK Base Station

Project Overview

Portable GNSS RTK base station for drone-grade positioning.

Status: In development

Hardware Power Wiring Status

GitHub (soon) Notion (soon)

At a Glance

What: a ground reference station broadcasting RTK corrections to a rover (e.g., a drone) to achieve centimeter-level accuracy.

Goals: compact modular enclosure, integrated Li-ion power, quick tripod mount, and clean cabling using robust RF connections.

Use cases: flight tests, outdoor robotics, repeatable positioning during experiments.

Main Features

GNSS Front-End

Here4Base (u-blox F9P) with a survey antenna (e.g., Harxon HX-CSX627A) mounted on top with a short high-quality coax.

Corrections Link

E220-900T22D long-range radio for RTK corrections when Wi-Fi/NTRIP isn’t available.

Field-Ready Power

Integrated Li-ion pack with charger & power-path, external USB-C, LEDs (Power/Fix/Radio).

Hardware

Controller

ESP32-C6 orchestrates serial links and system status while keeping the USB-C port accessible for service.

Enclosure

Modular layout for receiver, controller, radio, battery; bulkhead RF, clean strain-relief, quick tripod mount.

Robust Wiring

Short GNSS coax (LMR-200/RG-316), radio antenna offset from GNSS (≥20–30 cm), tidy harness inside.

The antenna is powered through the receiver’s internal bias via the coax.

Power

1S Li-ion pack with charger & power-path enables operation while charging. Two rails:

  • 5 V → Here4Base (F9P) and ESP32 VBUS
  • 3.3 V → E220 radio

Planned protections: PTC on USB-C input, TVS diodes, ferrites on power lines, and an LC filter on the 5 V rail to the receiver.

Wiring

Serial

  • ESP32-C6 UART1 ↔ Here4Base UART2 (RTCM/NMEA)
  • ESP32-C6 UART2 ↔ E220 (RTCM stream/control)
  • Common GND across all devices

RF

  • Short, quality GNSS coax
  • Radio antenna offset from GNSS
  • Strain-relief at pass-throughs

Layout

  • Separate switching regs from GNSS
  • Ground plane near receiver
  • Quick tripod mount hardware

Lightweight BOM

  • Survey antenna (e.g., Harxon HX-CSX627A) + short coax
  • Here4Base (u-blox ZED-F9P) receiver
  • ESP32-C6 DevKit
  • E220-900T22D radio + SMA antenna
  • 1S Li-ion pack (6–10 Ah), charger & power-path, boost 5 V, buck 3.3 V
  • Tripod mount hardware, enclosure, cable glands / RF bulkheads

A full, part-numbered BOM will follow once the mechanical layout is finalized.
